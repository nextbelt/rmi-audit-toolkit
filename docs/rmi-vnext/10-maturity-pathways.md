## 1. Purpose

Maturity Pathways describe **exactly what changes between maturity levels** for each domain and subdomain. They answer: "What does Level 3 look like in Work Management? What must change to reach Level 4?"

---

## 2. Maturity Level Definitions (Universal)

| Level | Name             | Core Characteristic                 | Management Behavior         | Data Usage                                |
|-------|------------------|-------------------------------------|-----------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| 1     | **Reactive**     | Firefighting; run-to-failure        | "Fix it when it breaks"     | No data; paper or tribal knowledge        |
| 2     | **Emerging**     | Some process; inconsistent          | "We have some procedures"   | Basic CMMS entry; minimal reporting       |
| 3     | **Systematic**   | Standardized; documented            | "We follow our process"     | KPIs tracked; reports reviewed monthly    |
| 4     | **Proactive**    | Data-driven; optimized              | "Data tells us what to do"  | Analytics driving decisions; trending     |
| 5     | **Prescriptive** | World-class; continuously improving | "We anticipate and prevent" | Predictive models; automated optimization |

## 3. Domain Pathways

### 3.1 Workforce Capability (WC)

#### WC.1 — Technical Competency

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                                                  | Key Indicators                                                    |
|-------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No formal training; technicians learn by watching. No skills tracking.                                                              | No training records; can't identify skill gaps                    |
| 2     | Some OJT; experienced techs train new hires informally. Basic safety training exists.                                               | Safety training records; informal mentoring                       |
| 3     | Skills matrix exists covering critical equipment. Annual training plan. Documented SOPs for key tasks.                              | Skills matrix >70% complete; training budget approved             |
| 4     | Competency assessments tied to equipment assignments. Training effectiveness measured. Certification tracking.                      | Formal competency assessments; training ROI tracked               |
| 5     | Continuous competency development. Cross-training for flexibility. Industry certifications standard. Predictive skill gap analysis. | 100% certified on assigned equipment; zero skill-related failures |

#### WC.2 — Training & Development

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                                | Key Indicators                                                 |
|-------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No training budget. Training = "follow Joe around."                                                               | Zero training hours logged                                     |
| 2     | Some vendor training. Training happens when equipment is new. No plan.                                            | <20 hours/tech/year; no needs analysis                         |
| 3     | Annual training plan with budget. Mix of classroom and hands-on. Records maintained.                              | 40+ hours/tech/year; training plan aligned to skill gaps       |
| 4     | Blended learning (e-learning + hands-on + simulation). Training effectiveness measured by on-the-job performance. | Training tied to competency assessments; effectiveness metrics |
| 5     | Personalized development paths. AI-recommended training. Industry-leading investment. Knowledge sharing culture.  | >80 hours/tech/year; zero repeat failures from skill gaps      |

#### WC.3 — Knowledge Management

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                                            | Key Indicators                                                 |
|-------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | All knowledge in people's heads. When experts retire, knowledge is lost.                                                      | No documentation; "ask Bob" culture                            |
| 2     | Some SOPs exist but outdated. CMMS has basic asset info. No mentoring program.                                                | <20% of procedures documented                                  |
| 3     | Active SOP program. Equipment manuals digitized and accessible. Mentoring pairs established.                                  | >60% SOPs current; digital document management                 |
| 4     | Knowledge base searchable by anyone. Lessons learned captured from every major event. Knowledge transfer plans for key roles. | Searchable knowledge base; succession plans for critical roles |
| 5     | AI-assisted knowledge retrieval. Automatic SOP generation from best-practice data. Community of practice active.              | Real-time knowledge access; zero knowledge-loss incidents      |

### 3.2 Leadership & Culture (LC)

#### LC.1 — Management Commitment

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                                      | Key Indicators                                                       |
|-------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | Maintenance = cost center to minimize. No dedicated budget line. Understaffed.                                          | Maint budget lumped with production; staffing below 1:20 ratio       |
| 2     | Maintenance budget exists but first to be cut. Some management awareness of reliability.                                | Budget exists but <80% of need; reliability mentioned in meetings    |
| 3     | Protected maintenance budget. Manager-level reliability champion. Maintenance represented in capital planning.          | Budget >90% funded; reliability on leadership agenda monthly         |
| 4     | Reliability metrics in executive dashboards. Maintenance viewed as value creator. Investment in reliability technology. | Executive KPI review includes MTBF/OEE; dedicated improvement budget |
| 5     | Asset management integrated into corporate strategy. Board-level asset management governance.                           | AM policy signed by C-suite; reliability in annual report            |

#### LC.2 — Safety & Reliability Culture

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                          | Key Indicators                                                 |
|-------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | Blame culture. Near-misses hidden. Production always overrides safety.                                      | No near-miss reports; stop-work authority doesn't exist        |
| 2     | Safety rules exist but inconsistently enforced. Technicians feel pressure to cut corners.                   | Safety meetings held but minimal participation                 |
| 3     | Active safety program. Stop-work authority established and used. Near-miss reporting encouraged.            | >5 near-miss reports/month; zero reprisals for stop-work       |
| 4     | Just culture implemented. Safety leading indicators tracked. Proactive hazard identification.               | Leading indicators trending positive; peer safety observations |
| 5     | Safety and reliability fully integrated. Industry-leading safety record. Culture of continuous improvement. | Zero lost-time incidents; safety culture survey >90% positive  |

#### LC.3 — Organizational Structure

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                                              | Key Indicators                                              |
|-------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No dedicated maintenance organization. Technicians report to production. No planners.                                           | Maintenance = "whoever is available"                        |
| 2     | Maintenance supervisor exists. Some functional separation from production. No planners.                                         | Dedicated maint supervisor; org chart exists                |
| 3     | Dedicated planning function. Maintenance reports independently (not to production). Defined roles and responsibilities.         | 1 Planner per 15-20 techs; RACI matrix exists               |
| 4     | Reliability Engineering function established. Maintenance and operations collaborate formally.                                  | Dedicated RE; joint M&O planning meetings                   |
| 5     | Integrated asset management organization. Centers of excellence for reliability. Cross-functional continuous improvement teams. | CoE established; cross-functional teams driving improvement |

### 3.3 Work Management (WM)

#### WM.1 — Planning & Scheduling

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                      | Key Indicators                                            |
|-------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No planning. Work done on demand. "Whoever's closest, go fix it."                                       | 0% planned work; no job plans                             |
| 2     | Some PMs scheduled. Corrective work done ad-hoc. Basic priority system (high/med/low).                  | <30% planned work; PM calendar exists                     |
| 3     | Formal planning process. Job plans for PMs and top corrective tasks. Weekly scheduling.                 | >60% planned work; >80% schedule compliance               |
| 4     | Multi-week scheduling. Data-driven estimates. Kitting for all planned work. Backlog managed to target.  | >80% planned work; >90% schedule compliance               |
| 5     | Optimized scheduling with constraint modeling. Predictive trigger integration. Near-zero reactive work. | >90% planned work; >95% schedule compliance; <5% reactive |

#### WM.2 — Preventive/Predictive Maintenance

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                                       | Key Indicators                                                 |
|-------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | Run to failure. No PM program.                                                                                           | 0% PM compliance (no PMs exist)                                |
| 2     | Basic PM program. Time-based tasks (oil changes, filter replacements). No optimization.                                  | PM program exists; <60% compliance                             |
| 3     | PM program covers critical assets. Some condition-based tasks (vibration, temperature). PM tasks reviewed for value.     | >80% PM compliance; PM program reviewed annually               |
| 4     | PdM technologies deployed on critical assets. RCM analysis performed on bad actors. PM/PdM tasks optimized.              | PdM covering >50% of critical assets; RCM on top 20 bad actors |
| 5     | Full PdM integration with CMMS. Automatic WO generation from condition alerts. Reliability-centered maintenance culture. | PdM >80% of critical assets; <5% PM tasks not adding value     |

#### WM.3 — Work Execution & Quality

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                               | Key Indicators                                            |
|-------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No SOPs. No quality checks. LOTO compliance unknown.                                                             | No SOPs; LOTO violations common                           |
| 2     | SOPs exist for some tasks. LOTO procedure documented but compliance varies.                                      | SOPs for safety-critical tasks; LOTO audits occasional    |
| 3     | SOPs for all PM tasks. LOTO compliance audited regularly. Parts kitted for planned work. First-time fix tracked. | >80% SOP usage; 100% LOTO compliance; first-time fix >70% |
| 4     | Job quality reviews performed. Repeat failures investigated. Craft feedback loop to improve job plans.           | First-time fix >85%; repeat failure rate <10%             |
| 5     | Zero-defect maintenance culture. Precision maintenance practices standard. Continuous craft improvement.         | First-time fix >95%; precision alignment/balance standard |

### 3.4 Asset Information (AI)

#### AI.1 — CMMS/EAM Effectiveness

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                | Key Indicators                                                |
|-------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No CMMS. Paper work orders or nothing.                                                            | No digital work management system                             |
| 2     | CMMS installed but underutilized. Used for WO creation only. Desktop-only access.                 | CMMS exists; <50% of work in system                           |
| 3     | CMMS standard for all work. Mobile access. WO workflow enforced. Master data reasonably clean.    | >90% work in CMMS; mobile deployed; WO workflow active        |
| 4     | CMMS integrated with procurement, HR, finance. Advanced workflows. KPI dashboards from CMMS data. | Full system integration; automated KPI reporting              |
| 5     | AI-enhanced CMMS. Natural language WO creation. Auto-classification. Predictive WO generation.    | AI features active; system drives decisions, not just records |

#### AI.2 — Data Quality & Integrity

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                                     | Key Indicators                                       |
|-------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No failure data. WOs closed with "fixed" or "complete." No asset hierarchy.                                            | <10% WOs with failure codes                          |
| 2     | Failure codes exist but generic. Asset hierarchy flat. Data quality not measured.                                      | Failure code list exists; <50% WO closure quality    |
| 3     | ISO 14224-aligned failure codes. 3+ level asset hierarchy. WO closure standards enforced.                              | >70% WO closure quality; 3+ level hierarchy          |
| 4     | Failure data analyzed for patterns. Asset hierarchy covers all maintainable equipment. Data quality audited quarterly. | >90% WO closure quality; quarterly data audits       |
| 5     | Automated data quality monitoring. ML failure pattern recognition. Industry-benchmarked data integrity.                | >98% WO closure quality; automated anomaly detection |

#### AI.3 — Analytics & Decision Support

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                             | Key Indicators                                              |
|-------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No reports. No dashboards. Decisions based on gut feeling.                                     | No analytics capability                                     |
| 2     | Basic CMMS reports (open WOs, PM list). Excel analysis. Ad-hoc reporting.                      | Monthly reports generated manually                          |
| 3     | Standard dashboard with key KPIs. Bad actor reports available. Regular reporting cadence.      | Live dashboard; bad actor report in <5 minutes              |
| 4     | Advanced analytics (trend analysis, Pareto, Weibull). Decision support tools. Data-driven RCA. | Predictive models for top failure modes                     |
| 5     | Prescriptive analytics recommending actions. Real-time dashboards. ML models in production.    | AI recommending maintenance actions; real-time optimization |

### 3.5 Strategy & Governance (SG)

#### SG.1 — Asset Management Policy

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                               | Key Indicators                                      |
|-------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No AM policy. No documented strategy. Maintenance is purely tactical.                                            | No written policy                                   |
| 2     | Informal understanding of maintenance goals. Some annual objectives.                                             | Verbal goals; no formal documentation               |
| 3     | Written AM policy aligned to organizational objectives. Risk register maintained. Asset criticality rankings.    | AM policy document; criticality assessment complete |
| 4     | AM policy reviewed annually. ISO 55001 alignment assessed. AM objectives cascade to department level.            | Annual policy review; ISO gap analysis completed    |
| 5     | ISO 55001 certified or equivalent. AM policy integrated into corporate governance. Continuous improvement cycle. | ISO 55001 certified; AM in corporate strategic plan |

#### SG.2 — Performance Measurement

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                 | Key Indicators                                       |
|-------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No maintenance KPIs. Cost tracked at plant level only.                                             | No maintenance-specific metrics                      |
| 2     | Some KPIs (PM compliance, backlog). No targets. Reviewed occasionally.                             | 2-3 KPIs tracked; no formal review                   |
| 3     | Defined KPI framework (leading + lagging). Monthly review with targets. OEE tracked for key lines. | 8+ KPIs with targets; monthly review meetings        |
| 4     | KPIs benchmarked against industry. Statistical analysis of trends. KPIs drive resource allocation. | Benchmarked KPIs; statistical control charts         |
| 5     | Balanced scorecard approach. Predictive KPIs. KPIs directly linked to business outcomes.           | KPIs linked to EBITDA; predictive performance models |

#### SG.3 — Continuous Improvement

| Level | What It Looks Like                                                                                           | Key Indicators                                               |
|-------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | No RCA process. Same failures repeat. No improvement tracking.                                               | No RCA; repeat failure rate unknown                          |
| 2     | RCA performed on catastrophic failures only. Some action tracking.                                           | Ad-hoc RCA; <50% actions completed                           |
| 3     | Formal RCA process (5-Why, fishbone). Action tracking system. Improvement projects defined annually.         | >80% RCA actions completed; annual improvement plan          |
| 4     | RCA on all significant failures. Cross-functional improvement teams. Improvement ROI tracked.                | RCA on all bad actors; improvement ROI positive              |
| 5     | Proactive failure elimination. Continuous improvement embedded in culture. Improvement sharing across sites. | Failure rate declining year-over-year; best practice sharing |

## 5. Typical Maturity Profiles

### 5.1 "The Firefighter" (RMI 1.5-2.0)
```
WC: 1.8  |  LC: 1.5  |  WM: 1.7  |  AI: 1.2  |  SG: 1.0
```
- No CMMS, no planning, no strategy
- Focus: Get CMMS running (AI.1), basic PM program (WM.2), stop-work authority (LC.2)

### 5.2 "The Aspiring" (RMI 2.5-3.0)
```
WC: 2.8  |  LC: 2.5  |  WM: 3.0  |  AI: 2.2  |  SG: 2.0
```
- PM program running but data quality poor
- Focus: Data quality (AI.2), planning function (WM.1), AM policy (SG.1)

### 5.3 "The Solid Performer" (RMI 3.2-3.8)
```
WC: 3.5  |  LC: 3.4  |  WM: 3.8  |  AI: 3.0  |  SG: 3.3
```
- Good processes, now needs data-driven optimization
- Focus: PdM deployment (WM.2), analytics (AI.3), KPI benchmarking (SG.2)

### 5.4 "The Leader" (RMI 4.0-4.5)
```
WC: 4.2  |  LC: 4.0  |  WM: 4.3  |  AI: 4.1  |  SG: 3.8
```
- Advanced practices, SG often lags
- Focus: ISO 55001 certification (SG.1), prescriptive analytics (AI.3), CI culture (SG.3)
